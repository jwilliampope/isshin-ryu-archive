Shimabuku's Early Life

He was born to a farming family in 1908. Working the farm gave him strength and discipline, which he would employ to good results in his study of Shorin, Goju, and Kobudo karate.

The Growth of a Legend

Among his teachers was the formidable Motubu Choki (left, giving instruction, early 20th century). Tatsuo became Choki's most famous and respected student.

The War Years

He was impressed into forced labor by the Japanese during the war, but his spirit remained unbroken. His struggles firmed his resolve to bring a new conception of karate to the world.

Isshin-Ryu In America

Tatso Shimabuku came to America in 1964 and 1966. His demonstrations were legendary, particularly the Cleveland conference of 1966. His students included Don Nagle, Howard Long, and Steve Armstrong, among others. These disciples went on to open their own dojos throughout the land, and taught countless students the "One-Hearted Way."


Shimabuku died in 1975. His disciples carried Isshin-Ryu to subsequent generations, most notably in America, where it exploded in popularity. Masters and acolytes of Isshin-Ryu regularly make pilgrimages to Kinaka, Gushikawa, Okinawa, where Tatsuo is buried.
